Cultural Reflection Blog 1: Reflection

Question 1: what's your take on the EDA experience?

What this video has clarified for me is that EDA is a rigorous approach to team-driven learning, teaching skills rather than (or perhaps alongside) tools.

Question 2: What are your impressions?

Instinctually I sense that we will all be challenged in a multitude of ways during this process. I took special note of the "deny yourself security of numbers" that Sherif brought up related to graduation and employment rates.

Question 3: How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

I'm really excited to get more in the "kitchen rather than table" approach to learning. Heirarchical learning has brought me elements of stress, comparison, and egoism throughout the years, particularly as a teacher. I am very enthusiastic to explore this new learning style, which requires both effort and humility from us all.

Question 4: Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?

Changed isn't the word I'd use. I'd say they've deepened -- the intentions and values I've witnessned at play at EDA were expanded and deepened throughout Sherif's video.

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

To quote Ming (and Amy Poehler), "yes please!" Certainly I am excited for this new chapter, experience, and style... and you bet your bottom dollar I've got nerves! But working through them, observing my mind, and developing a more friendly relationship with this experience of discomfort is already proving a rewarding journey.

kia ora!
