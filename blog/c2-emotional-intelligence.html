How did you find the two minutes of mindfulness?


When I first started practicing mindfulness, I'll be honest, it felt like a chore. Not only was it difficult to muster the motivation or intentional time to practice, but the truth is I really did not like experiencing my own mind. It was cluttered, frightened, and anxious. I frequently found my mind wandering into worry, bad outcomes, and worst case scenarios. It wasn't fun! But the more I've spent time exploring mindfulness, the more I've come to befriend the fear, the hurt, and the pain that created those patterns of thinking. The more I practice, the more I can have an objective experience ("the watcher") of my experience ("phenomena"). Meng describes this distance in both humorous and scientific approaches. I'm grateful that after some years of practice, I know this slow-build sense of soothing awareness to be deeply true in my personal experience. These two minutes have become like wading into a cool pool on a hot day. What an oasis that friendliness can be!
